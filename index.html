An Overview of Australian Wheat Production

Australia is a largescale agricultural producer, and one of its biggest products in the industry is wheat.

Australian Statistics

Australia is the second-driest continent after Antarctica, and soils are generally nutrient poor, making the majority of it's land unsuitable for intensive agriculture and yields poorer than other countries around the world. However, Australia's land has become more productive over time due to increased fertiliser use and advances in equipment, reflected in the t/ha produced in each state increasing over time. Tasmania's yield of 5.6 t/ha in 2020 is the highest ever recorded in an Australian state.

Wheat accounted for 75% of all crop production in the mid 1970s, however producers have since begun diversifying their agriculture, with products such as chickpeas and canola entering the market.

Wheat is Australia's highest value crop, worth over 12.3 Billion AUD in 2020. While wheat production has grown over the years, it's value as a proportion of all crops produced has decreased, likely due to a surge in the production of canola due to it's high price and global demand as a result of shortages in other countries.
