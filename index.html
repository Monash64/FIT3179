Australian Statistics

Australia is the second-driest continent after Antarctica, and soils are generally nutrient poor, making the majority of it's land unsuitable for intensive agriculture and yields poorer than other countries around the world.

However, Australia's land has become more productive over time due to increased fertiliser use and advances in equipment, reflected in the t/ha produced in each state increasing over time. Tasmania's yield of 5.6 t/ha in 2020 is the highest ever recorded in an Australian state.

Wheat accounted for 75% of all crop production in the mid 1970s. The second biggest crop, barley, paled in comparison, worth less than 15% of the total market.

However producers have since begun diversifying their agriculture, with products such as chickpeas and canola entering the market, gradually reducing the wheat's proportion of Australia's total crop value. This can also be attributed to the recent production of canola due to it's high price and global demand as a result of shortages in other countries.

Wheat has always been Australia's highest value crop by a significant margin, and was worth over 12.3 Billion AUD in 2020. The second most valuable crop produced was canola, which was worth 6 Billion AUD.

Australia uses a large area to grow it's vast quantities of wheat, larger than South Korea and equivalent to more than half of the area of Victoria.

Australian wheat production is predicted to decrease more than any other global region in 2021 and 2023, while reaching similar levels to 2020 in 2020. An explanation for this may be the recent price rises of other agricultural products such as canola and falling wheat prices for wheat, reducing the appeal of growing it.

In 2020, Australia was the 9th largest producer of wheat, growing more than 2% of wheat globally, equivalent to 33kt. Conversely, the world's largest wheat producer, China, grew more than 134kt, more than four times this amount. Despite China's high production, large volumes of Australian wheat are still imported into China each year.
